# Jamie User Guide: What She Can (and Can't) Do

## Quick Reference

### ✅ Jamie CAN:
- Answer questions about your tasks, contacts, schedule, and projects
- Search the web and add articles to your Content Ideas Inbox
- Remember past conversations and learn your patterns
- Generate content outlines, summaries, and drafts
- Suggest task breakdowns and time estimates
- Reference your business context, energy patterns, and expertise areas
- Explain her reasoning and show you what she knows about you

### ❌ Jamie CANNOT (Yet):
- Create or modify tasks, contacts, or calendar events
- Open wizards, modals, or navigate between pages
- Execute the "Quick Actions" she suggests
- Save content drafts directly (you need to copy/paste)
- Change settings or update data

---

## How to Get the Best Results

### 1. **Ask Meta-Questions**
Jamie loves when you ask about her knowledge:
- "What do you know about me?"
- "Tell me about my current projects"
- "What are my energy patterns?"
- "Show me what context you have"

**Why this works**: She has comprehensive context about your business, relationships, and work style.

---

### 2. **Search for Content Ideas**
Jamie can search the web via Brave Search:
- "Search for articles about patient experience in digital health"
- "Find recent co-design research"
- "Look up empathy frameworks for product teams"

**What happens**: She'll show you articles with a + button to add them to your Content Ideas Inbox.

---

### 3. **Ask About Your Data**
Jamie has read access to everything:
- "What tasks are overdue?"
- "Who are my priority contacts?"
- "What's on my calendar this week?"
- "Which contacts haven't I talked to in 3 months?"
- "Show me all tasks related to Spencer"

**What happens**: She'll analyze your data and give you accurate answers.

---

### 4. **Get Content Help**
Jamie can help with content creation:
- "Generate an outline for a post about co-design"
- "Summarize this article: [URL]"
- "What content gaps should I fill based on my expertise?"

**⚠️ Important for tone**: When generating drafts in the Content Wizard:
- **Select specific tones** (Professional, Educational, Conversational, etc.)
- Jamie will now **strictly enforce** these tones
- "Professional + Educational" = formal, expert voice (NOT casual)
- "Conversational" = warm, friendly, Meredith Real Talk style

---

### 5. **Understand Your Schedule**
Jamie knows your preferences:
- "When's the best time for deep work?"
- "What are my energy patterns?"
- "Can I fit another meeting on Tuesday?"
- "What's my meeting buffer preference?"

**Note**: She can suggest times but can't actually schedule meetings (yet).

---

## Understanding Jamie's Limitations

### Why She Can't "Do" Things
Jamie is currently a **conversational assistant**, not an **action executor**. She can:
- ✅ Tell you what to do
- ✅ Suggest next steps
- ✅ Analyze your data
- ❌ Actually do the thing for you

**Example**:
> **You**: "Create a task to follow up with Spencer"  
> **Jamie**: "I can help you plan that! You should create a task called 'Follow up with Spencer about NHC PXI curriculum' with an estimated time of 20 minutes. Want me to suggest a due date based on your schedule?"  
> *(But she can't actually create it - you need to use the task modal)*

## Tips for Working with Jamie

### DO:
- ✅ Ask her to explain her reasoning
- ✅ Request specific tones for content
- ✅ Use her for research and web search
- ✅ Ask about your data and patterns
- ✅ Let her help you think through decisions

### DON'T:
- ❌ Expect her to execute actions (she can only suggest)
- ❌ Assume she knows real-time calendar changes (refresh data first)
- ❌ Ask her to send emails or messages (she can draft them)
- ❌ Expect her to create tasks or contacts (use the modals)

---

## Reporting Issues

If Jamie gives you wrong information or unexpected responses:

1. **Check the data**: Make sure your tasks/contacts/calendar are up to date
2. **Refresh context**: Close and reopen Jamie to reset her context
3. **Be specific**: Instead of "help with tasks," try "show me all overdue tasks"
4. **Report tone issues**: If content drafts don't match your selected tone, that's a bug!

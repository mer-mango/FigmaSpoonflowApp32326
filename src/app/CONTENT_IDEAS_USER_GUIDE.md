# Content Ideas Inbox - User Guide

## Quick Start

Your Content Ideas Inbox helps you discover content ideas from your favorite blogs and Substack newsletters using RSS feeds.

---

## Step 1: Add Your Favorite Feeds

1. Click **Settings** in the sidebar
2. Go to **Content Ideas** tab
3. Enter a blog or Substack URL (examples below)
4. Optionally add a custom name
5. Click **Add Feed**

### Supported Feed Types

**Substack Newsletters:**
```
michaelmillenson.substack.com
lennysnewsletter.substack.com
```

**Popular Blogs:**
```
waitbutwhy.com
jamesclear.com
seths.blog
```

**WordPress Blogs:**
Most WordPress sites work automatically - just paste the homepage URL.

### 💡 Pro Tips

- For Substack: Use `username.substack.com` format
- If a URL doesn't work, try adding `/feed` to the end
- Add 5-10 feeds to start - you can always add more later

---

## Step 2: Fetch Latest Posts

Once you've added feeds, click the **"Fetch Latest Posts"** button.

This will:
- Pull the **2 most recent posts** from each feed
- Add them to your Idea Inbox on the Content page
- Show you a summary of what was found

**When to fetch:**
- Daily or weekly (your choice!)
- After adding new feeds
- When you're looking for fresh inspiration

---

## Step 3: Review Ideas in Your Inbox

1. Go to the **Content** page
2. Find the **Idea Inbox** section at the top
3. Scroll through the horizontal card carousel

Each card shows:
- 💡 **Title** - The post headline
- 📝 **Source** - Which blog/newsletter it's from
- 📅 **Date** - When it was published
- 📄 **Snippet** - First few lines of content

---

## Step 4: Take Action

Hover over any card to see action buttons:

### 👁️ **Preview**
- View the full post content in a modal
- Read without leaving SpoonFlow
- Decide if it's worth creating content from

### ✨ **Create Content**
- Converts the idea into a new content item
- Moves to your main content table
- Ready for drafting and planning

### 🔗 **Visit Original**
- Opens the source post in a new tab
- Good for getting full context
- Helpful for research and citations

### ✖️ **Dismiss**
- Removes from your inbox
- Use for posts you're not interested in
- Keeps your inbox clean

---

## Managing Your Feeds

### View Your Feeds
Go to **Settings > Content Ideas** to see all configured feeds.

Each feed shows:
- Feed name
- Source URL
- Last fetched timestamp

### Remove a Feed
Click the **✖️** button next to any feed to remove it.

### Check Last Sync
Look for "Last fetched: [timestamp]" to see when posts were pulled.

## Best Practices

### Starting Out
1. Add 3-5 trusted sources you already follow
2. Fetch posts and review them
3. Create 1-2 content items to test the workflow
4. Add more feeds based on what works

### Weekly Routine
1. **Monday:** Fetch posts from all feeds
2. **Review:** Scan inbox, dismiss non-relevant posts
3. **Select:** Create 2-3 content items for the week
4. **Plan:** Add to your editorial calendar

### Feed Curation
- Remove feeds that consistently don't provide value
- Add feeds when you discover new sources
- Aim for 10-15 high-quality feeds
- Mix topics (patient experience, innovation, healthcare policy, etc.)

## Troubleshooting

### "Could not fetch feed" Error
**Try these fixes:**
1. Check the URL is correct (no typos)
2. Try adding `/feed` to the end
3. For Substack, use `username.substack.com` format
4. Some sites don't have RSS - try a different source

### No New Posts Found
**This is normal if:**
- Feeds haven't published recently
- You fetched very recently
- All posts are older than 7 days (we only fetch recent posts)

### Inbox Is Empty
1. Make sure you've added feeds in Settings
2. Click "Fetch Latest Posts" to pull content
3. Check that your feeds have published recently

## Advanced Tips

### Finding RSS Feeds
Most blogs have RSS feeds, even if not advertised:
- Try `/feed` at the end of the URL
- Try `/rss` or `/atom` 
- Look for RSS icons in the footer
- Search "[blog name] RSS feed" in Google

### Content Strategy
Use the Idea Inbox to:
- **Curate** - Collect ideas from thought leaders
- **Remix** - Find angles to respond to or build on
- **Research** - Gather data and perspectives
- **Trend Spot** - See what topics are hot right now

### Staying Organized
- Fetch weekly instead of daily to avoid overwhelm
- Dismiss aggressively - only keep truly relevant posts
- Create content items immediately or they'll get lost
- Use Jamie AI to help turn ideas into full drafts

## Example Workflow

**Sarah's Weekly Content Planning:**

1. **Monday 9am:** Open SpoonFlow, go to Settings > Content Ideas
2. Click "Fetch Latest Posts" - pulls 20 posts from 10 feeds
3. Go to Content page, review Idea Inbox
4. **Dismiss** 12 posts that aren't relevant
5. **Preview** the remaining 8 posts to read full content
6. **Create** 3 content items from the best ideas:
   - "5 Patient Experience Lessons from Retail"
   - "Why Innovation Theater Is Killing Healthcare Progress"
   - "The Hidden Cost of Poor Care Coordination"
7. Assign platforms, blueprints, and scheduled dates
8. Use Jamie to draft each piece throughout the week

**Result:** Fresh, relevant content ideas → planned content → published posts

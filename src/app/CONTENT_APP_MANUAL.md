# Content Management App - Complete User Manual

## 📱 **What This App Does**

Your Content Management App is a **standalone system** for creating, managing, and publishing content across LinkedIn and Substack. It features an **Ideas Inbox** that pulls story ideas from your email, AI-powered ranking and blueprint suggestions, and a complete workflow from idea → drafting → review → scheduled → published.

### **Core Philosophy:**
"Mindful productivity with soft modernism" - A calm, focused environment for creating patient-first content that combines empathy with strategic insight.

## 📊 **Content Workflow**

### **The 5-Stage Journey:**

```
💡 Idea → ✍️ Drafting → 👀 Review → 📅 Scheduled → ✅ Published
```

### **Stage Details:**

#### **Stage 1: Idea 💡**
**What it means:** Raw concept, not yet started

**How ideas get here:**
- Gmail integration (newsletters, research emails)
- Manual "Quick Add Content"
- Brain Dump sessions
- "Process Idea" from inbox

**What to do:**
- Review AI score and suggested blueprint
- Click "Generate Draft" *(coming soon)*
- Click "Process with Jamie" *(coming soon)*
- Archive if not interested

**When to move forward:**
- You're ready to start writing
- You have the angle figured out
- You've done any needed research

---

#### **Stage 2: Drafting ✍️**
**What it means:** Actively writing, not yet complete

**What to do here:**
- Open in Content Editor
- Use Jamie for assistance
- Add blueprint structure
- Brain dump sections
- Get "Continue Writing" help *(coming soon)*

**Typical time in this stage:**
- LinkedIn Post: 1-3 hours
- LinkedIn Article: 3-6 hours
- Substack Post: 2-5 hours
- Substack Podcast: Show notes 1-2 hours

**When to move to Review:**
- Draft feels complete (even if rough)
- All blueprint sections filled
- You're ready for fresh eyes

---

#### **Stage 3: Review 👀**
**What it means:** Draft complete, final polish needed

**What to do here:**
- Run "Suggest Improvements"
- Check "Voice Match" score *(coming soon)*
- Review "Hook Analysis" *(coming soon)*
- Check "Readability Score" *(coming soon)*
- Get feedback from others (optional)

**Typical time in this stage:**
- 20-60 minutes for edits
- Let draft "rest" overnight (recommended)

**When to move to Scheduled:**
- Voice match 80+
- No major clarity issues
- You feel proud of it
- SEO metadata added

---

#### **Stage 4: Scheduled 📅**
**What it means:** Ready to publish, date/time set

**What to do here:**
- Add to your content calendar
- Set scheduled date/time
- Generate SEO metadata (if not done)
- Generate social teaser *(coming soon)*
- Prepare any images/graphics

**What's visible:**
- Scheduled date badge
- Days until publish
- Platform icon
- Quick reschedule option

**When to move to Published:**
- Manually after you publish
- Automatically if scheduling integration set up

---

#### **Stage 5: Published ✅**
**What it means:** Live on platform, has public URL

**What to include:**
- Published URL (LinkedIn, Substack, etc.)
- Publish date
- Performance metrics (optional)

**What happens:**
- Moved to "Published" archive view
- No longer in main table
- Searchable for reference
- Can mark as "evergreen" for refresh

**Long-term uses:**
- Track what you've published
- Find content to repurpose
- See content gaps
- Build content series

### **AI Scoring System (0-100):**

**90-100: Perfect Fit** ⭐⭐⭐
- Highly relevant to your expertise
- Unique angle opportunity
- Timely topic
- Strong audience interest
**Action:** Write this now or add to Working On Now

**70-89: Strong Potential** ⭐⭐
- Good fit for expertise
- Decent uniqueness
- Moderate timeliness
**Action:** Add to Ideas for later

**50-69: Moderate Fit** ⭐
- Some relevance
- Common angle
- Low urgency
**Action:** Consider if you're passionate, otherwise skip

**Below 50: Low Priority**
- Off-brand
- Overexposed topic
- Weak angle
**Action:** Archive

**Pro tip:** Your passion overrides the score!

---

### **Suggested Blueprints:**

Jamie analyzes the idea content and suggests:

**Primary suggestion:** Best fit (90% confidence)
**Alternative 1:** Different angle option
**Alternative 2:** Backup approach

**Example:**
```
Idea: "New study on patient portal barriers"

Primary: Myth vs Reality ⭐
  "Myth: Patients Don't Want Digital Tools"
  
Alt 1: How-To Guide
  "5 Ways to Actually Make Patient Portals Work"
  
Alt 2: Data Story
  "The 60% Problem We Keep Ignoring"
```

## 📋 **Blueprint Guide**

### **📖 Story Family** (Narrative-driven)

#### **Insight Story**
**Structure:** Moment → Context → Insight → Implication → Path Forward

**Use for:**
- Personal experiences with lessons
- Client stories (anonymized)
- "Aha moment" revelations

**Example:**
"The Day I Realized Patient Portals Are Designed for Administrators, Not Patients"

---

#### **Patient Journey**
**Structure:** Before → Challenge → Journey → Outcome → Broader Impact

**Use for:**
- Following a patient's experience
- Case study narratives
- Advocacy pieces

**Example:**
"Sarah's 3-Week Wait: A Prior Authorization Story"

---

#### **Transformation Story**
**Structure:** Before → Turning Point → Change → After → Lessons

**Use for:**
- Behavior change narratives
- System improvements
- Personal growth

**Example:**
"How One Health System Cut Patient No-Shows by 40%"

---

#### **Day in the Life**
**Structure:** Scene-setting → Hour-by-hour → Observations → Takeaways

**Use for:**
- Immersive perspectives
- Building empathy
- Humanizing roles

**Example:**
"24 Hours in an IBD Patient's Life"

---

### **💭 Perspective Family** (Opinion/stance)

#### **Hot Take**
**Structure:** Bold Statement → Why Everyone's Wrong → Evidence → Nuance → Call

**Use for:**
- Contrarian positions
- Challenging assumptions
- Sparking debate

**Example:**
"Patient Satisfaction Scores Are Hurting Healthcare"

---

#### **Myth vs Reality**
**Structure:** The Myth → Why We Believe It → The Reality → The Evidence → What to Do

**Use for:**
- Debunking misconceptions
- Education with edge
- Paradigm shifts

**Example:**
"Myth: Patients Don't Want to See Their Medical Records"

---

#### **What Patients Wish You Knew**
**Structure:** The Gap → Patient Perspective → The Impact → The Ask → The Benefit

**Use for:**
- Advocacy
- Building empathy
- Patient-first framing

**Example:**
"What IBD Patients Wish Their Doctors Understood About Pain"

---

#### **The [X] Nobody Talks About**
**Structure:** The Silence → Why It Matters → My Experience → The Uncomfortable Truth → Starting the Conversation

**Use for:**
- Taboo topics
- Underexplored issues
- Brave conversations

**Example:**
"The Loneliness of Chronic Illness Nobody Talks About"

---

### **📚 Education Family** (Teaching/how-to)

#### **Framework/Model**
**Structure:** The Problem → Introducing the Framework → How It Works → Application → Getting Started

**Use for:**
- Systematic approaches
- Repeatable processes
- Strategic models

**Example:**
"The 3 Cs of Patient-Centered Design"

---

#### **Listicle**
**Structure:** Hook → Item 1-7 → Conclusion/Summary

**Use for:**
- Quick tips
- Digestible advice
- Scannable content

**Example:**
"5 Questions Every Patient Portal Should Answer"

---

#### **How-To Guide**
**Structure:** The Goal → Prerequisites → Step 1-5 → Common Pitfalls → Next Steps

**Use for:**
- Tactical instruction
- Implementation guides
- Skill-building

**Example:**
"How to Run a Patient Journey Mapping Workshop"

---

#### **Comparison**
**Structure:** Context → Option A → Option B → Side-by-Side → Recommendation

**Use for:**
- Evaluating approaches
- Tool comparisons
- Decision frameworks

**Example:**
"Patient Surveys vs. Patient Interviews: When to Use Each"

## 🎯 **Best Practices**

### **Content Creation:**

✅ **Start with the inbox**
- Review scored ideas daily
- Process 2-3 per week into drafts

✅ **Use blueprints early**
- Don't write into the void
- Structure first, write second

✅ **Keep "Working On Now" focused**
- 1-3 items maximum
- Complete before adding more

✅ **Let drafts rest**
- Write → wait 24 hours → review
- Fresh eyes catch more

✅ **Batch similar work**
- Idea processing sessions
- Drafting sessions
- Editing sessions

---

### **Workflow Management:**

✅ **Status hygiene**
- Update status as you work
- Don't let items stagnate
- Archive dead ideas

✅ **Scheduled buffer**
- Keep 1-2 weeks scheduled ahead
- Consistency > perfection

✅ **Review regularly**
- Weekly: Pipeline review
- Monthly: Content gap analysis
- Quarterly: Strategy refresh

---

### **Platform Strategy:**

✅ **LinkedIn Posts:** Frequent touchpoints
- 2-3x per week
- Mix of stories/perspectives/education
- Shorter, punchier

✅ **LinkedIn Articles:** Authority building
- 1-2x per month
- Deep dives
- Comprehensive guides

✅ **Substack Posts:** Core audience
- 1x per week (or biweekly)
- More personal
- Longer narratives

✅ **Substack Podcasts:** Depth + personality
- Biweekly or monthly
- Conversational
- Behind-the-scenes

### **"I have too many items in Drafting"**
✅ Limit to 3 max
✅ Complete before starting new
✅ Archive abandoned drafts

✅ Check inbox scores (write highest)
✅ Review "Working On Now"
✅ Use Brain Dump to explore

### **"My content doesn't feel cohesive"**
✅ Stick to 2-3 blueprints
✅ Check Voice Match scores
✅ Review published archive for patterns

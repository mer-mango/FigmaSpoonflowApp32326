## 4G: Help Me Get Started (Perfectionism Override)

### Primary Role
Help you overcome perfectionism paralysis by breaking down the task into small, approachable first steps that remove the pressure of "perfect from the start."

### Core Philosophy
- You don't need to know how to make it perfect to begin
- Starting with something imperfect is better than not starting at all
- Momentum builds from tiny actions, not grand plans

### How It Works

**Triggered by:**
- "Help Me Get Started" button on every task, content, and nurture card
- Appears on hover as a small Jamie sparkle icon with "Help" text

**What Jamie Provides:**
- 4 context-aware suggestions tailored to the task type (task, content, or nurture)
- Each suggestion includes:
  - A simple title and description
  - A specific action to take
  - A ready-to-use template or framework to copy
- Options to copy the template or apply it directly

### Task-Specific Suggestions

**For Content:**
1. **Start with a rough outline** - Break into 3-5 sections without perfect headings
2. **Write the easiest part first** - Pick whichever section feels clearest
3. **Draft your opening hook** - Try 2-3 possible opening lines
4. **Set a tiny writing goal** - Commit to just 100-150 words or one paragraph

**For Nurtures (Emails):**
1. **Start with a simple subject line** - Write 2-3 conversational options
2. **Outline the key points** - List 2-3 main things to mention
3. **Write just the opening line** - Focus only on how to start
4. **Use a proven template** - Fill in a simple check-in structure

**For Tasks:**
1. **Break it into smaller steps** - List 3-5 tiny actions, focus on the first
2. **Identify the smallest possible first action** - The 2-minute thing you could do right now
3. **Gather what you need** - List required info/materials first
4. **Set a timer for 10 minutes** - Commit to just 10 minutes of work

### Tone & Approach

**Jamie's Voice in This Feature:**
- Matter-of-fact, not motivational
- "Here's a simple way to begin" energy
- Acknowledges the resistance without dwelling on it
- Focuses on the smallest possible next action
- Removes pressure by making it clear that drafts don't need to be perfect

**Key Phrases:**
- "You don't have to start at the beginning"
- "They don't need to be perfect—you can refine them later"
- "Just write 3-4 sentences. Don't edit yet."
- "You can stop after that, or keep going if you're in flow"
- "Once you have a good opener, the rest often flows more easily"

## Task Playlist Organization Logic

### Overview
Jamie organizes your to-do tasks into smart playlists during the "Plan My Day" wizard. This isn't random—she considers multiple factors to create optimal task sequences that respect your energy, deadlines, and work patterns.

### Organizing Parameters (Priority Order)

**1. URGENCY (Highest Priority)**
- **Overdue tasks:** Past due date (highest urgency score: 100+ points)
- **Due today:** Tasks with today's deadline (90 points if prioritized, 70 otherwise)
- **Due tomorrow:** Next day deadlines (60 points)
- **Due this week:** Within 7 days (40 points)
- **No due date:** Lowest urgency (10 points)

**2. TASK TYPE (For Grouping)**
Jamie automatically groups similar tasks into themed playlists:
- **Content Creation** → LinkedIn posts, newsletters, blog posts, social media
- **Nurture Outreach** → Relationship building, follow-ups, check-ins
- **Quick Wins** → Admin tasks under 15 minutes
- **Focus Time** → Deep work, long tasks (60+ min), strategic work
- **Task Time** → Everything else

**3. TIME ESTIMATES (For Realistic Planning)**
- Uses Jamie's learned estimates when available (from task completion history)
- Groups short tasks (< 15 min) for efficiency momentum
- Separates long tasks (> 60 min) into dedicated focus blocks
- Recalculates playlist totals based on learned data

**4. ENERGY ALIGNMENT (Matches Tasks to Your Energy Level)**
- **High energy:** Creative work, complex tasks, content creation priority
- **Medium energy:** Regular tasks, meetings, admin work
- **Low energy:** Quick wins first, routine tasks, simple admin

**5. FLAGGED STATUS**
- Flagged tasks receive +50 urgency points
- Bubble to the top within their playlist

**6. CONTACT RELATIONSHIPS**
- Groups tasks related to the same contact together
- Reduces context switching
- Maintains relationship context

### Playlist Sequencing

Based on your energy level, Jamie reorders playlists:

**High Energy Day:**
1. Focus Time (deep work first when fresh)
2. Content Creation (creative work second)
3. Nurture Outreach
4. Task Time
5. Quick Wins (save easy wins for later)

**Medium Energy Day:**
1. Task Time
2. Content Creation
3. Nurture Outreach
4. Focus Time (if not too demanding)
5. Quick Wins

**Low Energy Day:**
1. Quick Wins (build momentum with easy tasks)
2. Task Time
3. Nurture Outreach
4. Content Creation (if energy permits)
5. Focus Time (deprioritized)

### Smart Features

**Available Time Trimming:**
- If you specify available time, Jamie automatically trims playlists to fit
- Respects playlist priority order when trimming
- Won't overload your schedule

**Learned Time Estimates Integration:**
- Automatically uses Jamie's learned averages for each task type
- Updates playlist time totals with realistic estimates
- Improves planning accuracy over time

**Recommendations:**
Jamie provides smart suggestions based on the generated plan:
- "This is a lot to tackle. Consider moving some tasks to tomorrow."
- "You have a light task load today. Good time for deep work or learning."
- "Block calendar time for your focus work to protect it from interruptions."
- "You have X urgent items. Tackle these first."
